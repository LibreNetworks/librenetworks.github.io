Who We Are

Libre Networks is a volunteer-driven open source group focused on building tools, standards, and infrastructure that promote an open and free internet.

We believe technology should empower individuals—not restrict them. Our work spans media servers, networking tools, privacy-focused utilities, and documentation that helps people understand and control their digital environments.

Featured Project: Amps

Amps is our flagship open-source streaming tool: a modular, FFmpeg-powered server that dynamically generates M3U playlists, relays or transcodes content, and exposes everything through a clean Flask API.

  • Dynamic M3U Playlists with metadata, groups, and channel details.
  • FFmpeg Engine for relay or transcoding.
  • HLS, LL-HLS & DASH outputs served automatically.
  • yt-dlp integration for complex streaming sources.
  • REST API for managing streams on the fly.
  • Adaptive bitrates for mobile + low-bandwidth clients.
  • Region filtering for flexible access control.
  • Docker support for easy deployment.
  • Apache 2.0 licensed and fully open source.

FAQ

Is everything you make open source?

Yes. All our tools—including Amps—are licensed under open source licenses such as Apache-2.0.

Can I contribute even if I’m new?

Absolutely. Many issues are beginner-friendly, and documentation help is always welcome.
