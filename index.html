Keeping the Web Free.

Our Mission

Libre Networks is a community-driven movement defending the open internet.
Around the world, websites and governments are increasingly restricting access—requiring IDs, deploying age-check AI, and limiting freedom. This approach is flawed and puts privacy, safety, and universal access at risk.
Major organizations like Wikipedia, 4chan, and Apple have challenged these restrictions in court, fighting for a safer, freer web.
Now, we’re asking you to stand with us.

Sign the Petition: Defend the Open Internet

We believe the web should be:

  • Accessible: No unnecessary barriers, ID checks, or invasive surveillance.
  • Private: Your browsing, identity, and conversations stay yours.
  • Open: No government or corporation should decide who gets to connect.

Sign our petition to demand:
  1. Protection against mandatory age or ID verification for general web use.
  2. Safeguards against AI-based surveillance and censorship.
  3. Transparent, community-driven standards for online safety and privacy.

Every signature sends a message to lawmakers, companies, and the world: keep the internet free and open for all.

Your data is never sold or shared. We only use signatures to demonstrate support for internet freedom.

Why This Matters

The web was built to connect people freely, not gatekeep or monitor. Forced ID checks and age AI threaten:

  • Access for marginalized users, privacy advocates, and those in restrictive countries.
  • Whistleblowers, activists, and regular people who just want safety and privacy.
  • The free flow of information and ideas that drives progress.

FAQ

Why is age/ID verification a problem?

While safety is important, blanket ID checks and AI age scans erode privacy and risk excluding many legitimate users—especially young people, activists, or those without easy access to official documents.

What happens with my signature?

We use signatures to demonstrate public opposition to restrictive laws and policies. No personal information is shared without consent.

How can I help?

Sign, share, contribute code, organize, translate, or reach out! Every voice counts.
